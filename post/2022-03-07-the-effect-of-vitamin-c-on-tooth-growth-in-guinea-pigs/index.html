The Effect of Vitamin C on Tooth Growth in Guinea Pigs

Justine Macalindong
Last updated on Mar 7, 2022 4 min read

Hi! In this post, I will be working with a data set from the ToothGrowth data set that is built-in with R. The data set is comes from an experiment in which 60 guinea pigs’s length of odontoblasts were measured. Each animal received one of three dose levels of vitamin C (0.5, 1, and 2 mg/day) by either orange juice or ascorbic acid.

ToothGrowth is a data frame consisting of 60 observations and 3 variables.

  • len Tooth length
  • supp factor Supplement type (VC or OJ)
  • dose Dose in milligrams/day

Next, I am grouping the data by the supplement type used and finding the mean length of the measured odonotoblasts of each supplement type.

df.supp <- ToothGrowth %>%
  group_by(supp) %>%
  summarise(avg = mean(len))
df.supp
## # A tibble: 2 x 2
##   supp    avg
##   <fct> <dbl>
## 1 OJ     20.7
## 2 VC     17.0

According to this, guinea pigs that were given the orange juice supplements had on average longer odontoblasts.

Now, I am grouping the data by the dosage level given and finding the mean length of the measured odonotoblasts of each dosage level.

df.dose <- ToothGrowth %>%
  group_by(dose) %>%
  summarise(avg = mean(len))
df.dose
## # A tibble: 3 x 2
##    dose   avg
##   <dbl> <dbl>
## 1   0.5  10.6
## 2   1    19.7
## 3   2    26.1

Guinea pigs that were given 2.0 mg/day of vitamin C had the longest odontoblasts on average. This shows guinea pigs that were given higher dosage treatment had longer ondontoblasts.

Finally, I am grouping together the dosage and supplement type and finding the average length of the guinea pig odontoblasts by treatment of each supplement type and dosage level.

df.sd <- ToothGrowth %>%
  group_by(supp, dose) %>%
  summarise(avg = mean(len))
## `summarise()` has grouped output by 'supp'. You can override using the `.groups` argument.

Guinea pigs that were given 2.0 mg/day of ascorbic acid had on average the longest measured odontoblasts (26.14). Therefore, according to this data, while attempting to maximize guinea pig odontoblast length, you should give them 2.0 mg/day of ascorbic acid.
